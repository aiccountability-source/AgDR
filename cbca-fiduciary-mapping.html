Fiduciary Mapping.

Autonomous agents do not absolve directors of their duties. AgDR v1.8 provides the evidentiary foundation for proving "Good Faith" and "Duty of Care" in the age of algorithmic decision-making.

CBCA s.122 Compliance

The Canada Business Corporations Act (CBCA) mandates that every director and officer must act honestly and in good faith with a view to the best interests of the corporation. When decisions are delegated to AI, that duty does not vanish—it intensifies. AgDR v1.8 maps these legal requirements to technical invariants.

CBCA s.122 Requirement AgDR v1.8 Technical Implementation
Honesty and Good Faith (s.122(1)(a)) The Purpose Pillar captures the explicit intent of every inference, proving the decision was made "with a view to the best interests of the corporation."
Duty of Care (s.122(1)(b)) The Human Delta Chain provides evidence of reasonable oversight, demonstrating that directors exercised the care, diligence, and skill of a reasonably prudent person.
Compliance with Regulations (s.122(2)) Automatic mapping to EU AI Act and AIDA within the record itself ensures the corporation is operating within the law.

The Fiduciary Shield

An AgDR record is more than a log; it is a Business Judgment Rule artifact. By proving that a system was operating within defined parameters (Place) and was overseen by a named human (FOI), directors can demonstrate they satisfied their fiduciary obligations even if a specific AI outcome was undesirable.

Closing the Accountability Gap

Without AgDR, an AI decision is a "black box" that creates existential liability for a board. With AgDR, it becomes a documented corporate action. The standard ensures that the Fiduciary Office Intervener (FOI) is the terminal point of every high-risk escalation, keeping humans in the loop where the law requires them to be.
