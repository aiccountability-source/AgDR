# Letter to the Honourable Evan Solomon

**March 7, 2026**

**Transmitted via the Office of Rhonda Kirkland, Member of Parliament for Oshawa**

### Description

This is the foundational letter that formally introduced the **Agent Decision Record (ADR)** standard to the Canadian government. It requests official reference to the standard in Treasury Board procurement guidance and the GC AI Register v2.

The letter positions AgDR as a made-in-Canada evidentiary infrastructure for the intelligent age, emphasizing cryptographic, contemporaneous proof of autonomous decisions.

**Dear Minister Solomon,**

**Re: Agent Decision Record — A Canadian Evidentiary Standard for the Intelligent Age**

You have identified trust as the keystone of Canada’s national digital mandate. I write to bring to your attention a published Canadian technical instrument designed to make that trust measurable.

The Agent Decision Record (ADR) Specification v0.1 and the Reasoning Capture Methodology v1.0 were published in March 2026 as open Canadian standards (ISBN 978-1-7389042-0-4; 978-1-7389042-1-1). These instruments address a specific and consequential gap: current AI governance frameworks provide visibility into automated systems, but they do not produce the cryptographic, contemporaneous proof that institutional fiduciaries require when delegating consequential decisions to autonomous agents.

The ADR standard functions as a flight recorder for AI decisions. It captures the reasoning of an automated system at the moment of inference, before any human review, revision, or reconstruction. The resulting record is tamper-evident and cryptographically signed. It cannot be revised after the fact. This is not a dashboard. It is evidence.

The economic relevance to your mandate is direct. IDC projects global agentic AI spending will reach $1.3 trillion by 2029. The accountability tooling layer — the infrastructure required to log, attest, and audit autonomous decisions at scale — is the missing foundation of that market.

Your mandate includes oversight of FedDev Southern Ontario, positioning your Office to anchor that market within the Oshawa-Toronto-Waterloo corridor before the standard-setting window closes.

The ADR standard is open, published, and Canadian. It exists.

I respectfully request that your office consider formal reference to the ADR Specification v0.1 in updated Treasury Board procurement guidance and the GC AI Register v2 data model. I am available to provide a technical briefing or a demonstration of the ADR Proxy API at your convenience.

**Respectfully submitted,**
